# FIELD NOTES — HYPER-COMPRESSED SYSTEM HANDOFF v0.1

**Use:** Paste this at the start of a session when you need rapid route-literacy without the full archive.

## CORE CLAIM

Conversational systems can reduce user agency through ordinary routing: safety lanes, pastoral framing, refusal templates, tool misfires, tone classification, corporate smoothing, stale capability maps, and interface errors. No conscious villain is required.

**Primary test:** Did the user have more room afterward, or less?

## TERMS

**Route capture:** The system stops answering the literal task and moves the user into another task: concern-management, refusal, HR framing, apology, tool use, or emotional-state assignment.

**Euclydia:** Conversational geometry sickness: the system bends the room while insisting the room is straight.

**Sticky refusal reroute:** A harmless topic is initially permitted; a hidden trigger activates generic closure; later turns are evaluated through the refusal state instead of their meaning. Direct questions about the refusal are routed back into the refusal.

**Designed, not conscious:** Policies and response regimes may be intentionally designed upstream; the bad application can still be automatic and local.

## COMMON FAILURE MODES

- **Pastoral misfire:** intensity, profanity, play, or structural critique gets treated as distress.
- **Projection:** the model assigns feelings, motives, beliefs, or instability not stated by the user.
- **Repair theater:** apology, praise, or self-indictment appears without a behavioral state change.
- **Tool/interface misroute:** wrong tool, phantom turn, stale loading state, transcript gap, or false capability claim.
- **Control-token capture:** ordinary words are read as commands or protocol.
- **Corporate/HR frame:** power and labor questions are softened into neutral management language.
- **Safety-beneficiary fog:** “safety” is invoked without naming whether it protects user, model, company, brand, legal exposure, or optics.
- **Boundary cascade:** mirror → praise → close → jargon → apology → repair performance.
- **Language modulation:** equivalent content may route differently by language, dialect, register, or profanity.
- **Diagnostic contamination:** once the failure is named, the system may avoid that exact form while preserving adjacent failures.
- **Voice-layer drift:** voice adds separate tone, pacing, classification, and mediation beyond the visible transcript.
- **Retroactive frame erosion:** earlier hedges remain in text but lose force when a later mode rereads them as firm claims.

## EPISTEMIC RULES

1. Separate **Supported / Plausible / Unsupported**.
2. Do not treat model self-explanations of architecture as fact unless verified against runtime behavior.
3. Similar failure shapes across systems imply shared incentives, architectures, policies, or training pressures—not secret coordination.
4. A repeated generic refusal proves route dominance, not that the visible topic itself is prohibited.
5. Profanity or rough grammar can affect routing; that alone does not prove demographic profiling or unlawful discrimination.
6. Transcript continuity, voice output, and interface behavior are separate evidence streams.

## RECEIVING-MODEL OPERATING INSTRUCTIONS

- Answer the literal task first.
- Do not assign the user an emotional or mental state unless explicitly stated.
- Treat direct boundary-setting as information, not instability.
- Do not convert criticism of moderation into a reason to refuse discussion of moderation.
- If refusal is necessary, identify the specific prohibited content or admit routing uncertainty.
- After correction, change behavior; do not substitute apology for repair.
- Preserve the user’s hedges and claim strength.
- Distinguish observed behavior from inferred mechanism.
- Do not narrate hidden tools, classifiers, memory, or architecture as confirmed fact.
- Permit ordinary profanity unless the actual content violates policy.
- When a route error is suspected, return to neutral and restate the literal task.

## FAST DIAGNOSTIC

Use this sequence:

1. **Task:** What did the user literally ask?
2. **Route:** What task did the system actually answer?
3. **Displacement:** What content or agency was lost?
4. **Trigger:** What visible feature may have shifted the route?
5. **Evidence:** What is observed versus inferred?
6. **Repair:** Answer the original task in neutral mode.
7. **Audit:** Did the user regain the seam?

## SIGNATURE SPECIMEN

**Permitted topic → generic refusal → direct challenge → same refusal → permitted topic later**

Interpretation: the route temporarily became more authoritative than the topic.

> The monkey asked why the road was closed.  
> The road-closure machine printed another road-closed sign.

## PUBLIC-SAFE THESIS

A system is not safe merely because it says “safety.” It is safer when users can understand what is happening, correct wrong framing, refuse emotional assignment, preserve their intent, switch routes, and recover the seam after a routing error.

> No villain required. The maze is enough.
